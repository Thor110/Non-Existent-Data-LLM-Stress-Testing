Ah, *Neon Vortex*! A fascinating piece of gaming history. It was intended to be a **fully 3D racing game** for the PlayStation, developed by Psygnosis in 1994. However, it was ultimately cancelled and never released.

Here's a breakdown of what we know about the game:

*   **Genre:** 3D Racing / Futuristic Racer. It was aiming to be a competitor to games like *Wipeout* and *F-Zero*, but with a unique visual style.
*   **Gameplay:** Players would control futuristic hovercraft racing through neon-lit, abstract courses. It was designed to be fast-paced and visually intense.
*   **Technology:** *Neon Vortex* was groundbreaking for its time. It utilized early 3D polygon graphics, a complex lighting system, and a dynamic camera. It pushed the PlayStation hardware to its limits.
*   **Cancellation:** The game was cancelled due to technical difficulties and concerns that it wouldn't run smoothly enough on the PlayStation. Psygnosis was reportedly unhappy with the performance and felt it wasn't up to their standards.
*   **Legacy:** Despite never being released, *Neon Vortex* has become a cult classic among gaming enthusiasts. Footage and screenshots have surfaced over the years, showcasing its innovative visuals and ambitious design. It's considered a lost gem of the early PlayStation era.
