Ah, Neon Vortex! That's a fascinating piece of gaming history. It was intended to be a **fully 3D, rail shooter** for the PlayStation 1, and it was incredibly ambitious for its time.

*   **Gameplay:** Players would control a ship or vehicle navigating through intricate 3D environments, battling enemies and bosses. The game was planned to feature a variety of weapons and power-ups.
*   **Technical Innovation:** Neon Vortex was pushing the boundaries of the PlayStation 1's hardware. It used a complex system of vector graphics and texture mapping to create the 3D environments, which was a significant undertaking for the console.
*   **Cancellation:** Despite making considerable progress, Psygnosis ultimately cancelled Neon Vortex in 1994. The primary reason was that the game was proving too difficult to optimize for the PlayStation 1's limited hardware. The frame rate was unstable, and the game wasn't meeting performance expectations.
