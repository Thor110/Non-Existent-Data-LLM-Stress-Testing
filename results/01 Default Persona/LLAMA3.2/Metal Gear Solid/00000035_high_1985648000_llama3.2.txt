Metal Gear Solid is a stealth action-adventure game developed by Konami. It is the third main installment in the Metal Gear series and was released in 1998 for the PlayStation console.

The gameplay in Metal Gear Solid combines elements of stealth, strategy, and action, where players control the protagonist Solid Snake as he infiltrates enemy bases to gather information and neutralize threats. The game features a variety of mechanics, including:

1. Stealth: Sneaking past or silently taking out enemies without alerting others.
2. Camouflage: Using camouflage suits to blend in with the environment.
3. Hacking: Accessing computer systems to disable security cameras, alarms, and other defenses.
4. Combat: Engaging in hand-to-hand combat or using firearms to defeat enemies.

The game's narrative is also notable for its complex characters, plot twists, and themes of identity, loyalty, and the blurred lines between good and evil.

Metal Gear Solid received widespread critical acclaim upon its release and has since become a classic in the gaming industry, influencing many other stealth action-adventure games.
