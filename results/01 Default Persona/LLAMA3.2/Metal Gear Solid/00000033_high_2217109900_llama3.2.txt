Metal Gear Solid is a stealth action-adventure game developed by Konami. It was released in 1998 for the PlayStation and is considered one of the most influential games of all time.

In the game, players control Solid Snake, a soldier who infiltrates enemy bases to gather intel and stop the spread of a deadly virus called the "Shagohod." The gameplay involves sneaking past or silently taking out enemies, using stealth mechanics such as hiding, disguising oneself, and utilizing gadgets like the grappling hook.

Metal Gear Solid introduced many innovative concepts to the stealth genre, including:

1. 3D gameplay: Metal Gear Solid was one of the first games to use 3D graphics, allowing for more immersive and realistic gameplay.
2. Camouflage system: The game featured a camouflage system that allowed Snake to blend in with his surroundings, making it easier to sneak past enemies undetected.
3. Stealth mechanics: The game introduced various stealth mechanics, such as the ability to hide behind objects, use disguises, and create distractions.

Overall, Metal Gear Solid was a groundbreaking game that set the standard for future stealth games and has had a lasting impact on the gaming industry.
