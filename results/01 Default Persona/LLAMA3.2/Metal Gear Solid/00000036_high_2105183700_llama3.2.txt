Metal Gear Solid is a stealth action-adventure game developed by Konami. It was released in 1998 for the PlayStation console and is considered one of the pioneering titles in the stealth genre.

In the game, players control Solid Snake, a covert operative who must infiltrate enemy territory to gather intelligence, neutralize threats, and complete objectives without being detected by the enemy. The gameplay involves sneaking around, avoiding detection, using camouflage and disguises, hacking security systems, and eliminating enemies through stealth or combat when necessary.

Metal Gear Solid introduced several innovations in game design, including:

1. 3D gameplay: Metal Gear Solid was one of the first games to utilize 3D graphics, providing a more immersive experience.
2. Stealth mechanics: The game's focus on stealth and strategy added a new layer of depth to action-adventure games.
3. Storytelling: The game's complex narrative and characters helped establish it as a classic in the gaming industry.

The success of Metal Gear Solid spawned numerous sequels, prequels, and spin-offs, cementing its place as one of the most iconic games of all time.
