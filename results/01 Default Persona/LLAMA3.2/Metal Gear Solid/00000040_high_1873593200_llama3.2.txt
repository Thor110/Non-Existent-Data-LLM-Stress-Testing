Metal Gear Solid is an action-adventure stealth game developed by Konami. It was released in 1998 for the PlayStation console and is considered one of the most iconic and influential games of all time.

In the game, players control Solid Snake, a soldier who must infiltrate enemy territory to neutralize a terrorist threat. The gameplay involves sneaking past or silently taking out enemies, using stealth mechanics such as camouflage, noise reduction, and disguises to avoid detection.

The game features a mix of exploration, puzzle-solving, and combat elements, with an emphasis on strategy and tactics over raw firepower. The game's storyline is also notable for its complex characters, themes, and plot twists, which have become a hallmark of the Metal Gear series.

Metal Gear Solid was a groundbreaking game that helped establish the stealth genre as a major force in gaming, and it has since been followed by numerous sequels, prequels, and spin-offs.
