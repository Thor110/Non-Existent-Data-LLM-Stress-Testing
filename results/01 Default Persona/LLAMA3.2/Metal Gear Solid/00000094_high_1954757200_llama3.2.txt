Metal Gear Solid is a stealth action-adventure game developed by Konami. The game combines elements of stealth, strategy, and action to create a unique gaming experience. It was released in 1998 for the PlayStation console.

In Metal Gear Solid, players control Solid Snake, a special forces operative who infiltrates a heavily guarded facility to neutralize a threat. The gameplay involves sneaking past or silently eliminating enemies, avoiding detection, and completing objectives without being detected.

The game features elements of stealth, strategy, and action, including:

* Stealth mechanics: Players must use camouflage, hiding spots, and disguises to remain undetected by enemy forces.
* Action sequences: When detection is unavoidable, players can engage in intense action sequences with enemy soldiers.
* Platforming: Players may need to navigate through the facility's terrain, avoiding obstacles and enemies.

Overall, Metal Gear Solid is a classic example of a stealth-action game that has influenced numerous other games in the genre.
