Metal Gear Solid is an action-adventure stealth video game developed by Konami. It was released in 1998 for the PlayStation console and is considered one of the most influential games of all time.

The gameplay in Metal Gear Solid involves sneaking, crawling, climbing, and using camouflage to evade enemies while trying to complete objectives. The player controls Solid Snake, a character who infiltrates enemy bases to gather information and neutralize threats.

Key features of the game include:

1. Stealth mechanics: Players must use stealth tactics to avoid detection by enemy forces.
2. Camouflage system: Snake can blend into his surroundings, making it harder for enemies to detect him.
3. Hacking: Players can hack security cameras, turrets, and other devices to gain access or disable them.
4. Upgrades and equipment: As the player progresses through the game, they can upgrade Snake's abilities and acquire new gear.

Overall, Metal Gear Solid is a classic example of an action-adventure stealth game that has had a significant impact on the gaming industry.
